The shift

Experience isn't correlated with the outcome.
It's how the outcome gets achieved.

  • Reduce tickets — how, if you haven't designed away the reason people file them?

The method for designing the XLA experience. The diagnostic, the four stages, the way we find the metric that moves. It runs every time — and produces a different, right-sized engagement each time.

The method is the asset. The diagnostic is the product. The engagement is its output.

So — to be clear about what you're buying

This isn't a template. That's the point.

Now that you've seen the method and the math, here's why it's priced to the outcome, not the calendar — and why that's the strength, not the catch.

  • Pre-sized engagements present as menu items. Packages may read as commodity — savvy buyers discount them. Premiums are paid on outcomes.
  • The pursuit team is tasked with designing the outcome — and the engagement that meets it.
  • That could be a $300K engagement. It could be a $3M one. The outcome decides, not a calendar.
Why pre-sizing would leave money on the table

A $20M offering sold at $300K a pop needs 66 engagements — at 8 weeks each, that's 530+ weeks ≈ a decade, or only ~$2M a year. Pre-sizing caps the program. The math doesn't math.
